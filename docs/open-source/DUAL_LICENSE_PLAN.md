# Dual License Plan

## Overview

Go-Fi should use a dual-license model:

* **Open Source License:** AGPL-3.0-only
* **Commercial License:** a separate paid license for closed-source, white-label, and enterprise use

This gives the community a real open-source project while preserving a path to revenue from businesses that want flexibility without AGPL obligations.

## Who Uses Which License

### AGPL-3.0-only

Use this for:

* community contributors
* self-hosted operators willing to comply with AGPL
* research, evaluation, and learning
* organizations comfortable publishing their modified source when required

### Commercial License

Use this for:

* companies that do not want to open source their modifications
* white-label deployments
* enterprise internal deployments that need private customization
* resellers embedding Go-Fi into a managed offering

## Example Commercial Terms

These are business terms, not legal boilerplate.

* single-tenant or multi-tenant commercial deployment rights
* right to modify without source disclosure obligations
* right to rebrand under the customer’s product name
* priority support during business hours or under SLA
* optional implementation assistance and migration services
* access to premium modules where separately licensed

## Suggested Commercial Package Structure

* **Startup License**: for one production deployment and limited support
* **Business License**: for larger deployments with standard support and branding flexibility
* **Enterprise License**: for multi-site, white-label, or managed service operators

## Pricing Logic

Price around business value, not code size.

Good pricing anchors:

* number of branches or hotspots
* number of active tenants
* support SLA level
* white-label rights
* deployment model: self-hosted, managed, or fully hosted

## Operational Rule

Keep the commercial offer simple:

* AGPL for the public core
* commercial license for private use cases
* support and hosting as separate revenue lines

That avoids legal complexity while preserving leverage.
