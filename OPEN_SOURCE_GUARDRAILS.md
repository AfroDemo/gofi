# Open Source Guardrails

## Things to Avoid When Open Sourcing This Project

- Do not switch to a permissive license like MIT if founder leverage matters.
- Do not allow unrestricted commercial reuse of official branding.
- Do not publish every future differentiator as free core functionality.
- Do not rely only on goodwill to protect monetization.
- Do not make financial logic impossible to audit.

## Mistakes That Kill Monetization

### 1. Weak License Choice
A permissive license can make cloning and reselling too easy.

### 2. No Trademark Protection
If forks can use your exact name and logo, your market position becomes weaker.

### 3. Giving Away Premium Value Too Early
If all differentiators are in the free repo, the hosted/commercial offering becomes harder to justify.

### 4. Ignoring Support as a Product
Many buyers pay for reliability, deployment, updates, and expertise — not only code.

### 5. No Clear Commercial Path
If people do not know how to buy a license or support, many opportunities are lost.

## Healthy Strategy

- strong copyleft for core
- protected branding
- premium roadmap
- clear commercial licensing
- paid implementation and support
